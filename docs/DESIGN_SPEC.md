# DESIGN_SPEC — Instagram Post: Damn

## Goal
One launch/brand post. Make people feel the studio is sharp and established. Drive to inquiry form.

## Format
- **Single square post**, 1080 × 1080 px, RGB, PNG export.
- Safe zone: keep text/logo 80 px inside all edges (avatar crops, UI overlays).

## Palette (4 colors only — hard constraint)
| Name   | Hex       | Use |
| ------ | --------- | --- |
| Black  | `#0A0A0B` | Background |
| Bone   | `#E5E5E5` | Primary text |
| Violet | `#7C3AED` | Accent / gradient |
| Cyan   | `#22D3EE` | Accent / gradient |

No other colors. Gradients = violet→cyan only.

## Layout
- Black background.
- **Headline** centered, big: `DAMN GOOD`. Bone. Heavy weight, tight tracking.
- `GOOD` (or one word) filled with violet→cyan gradient for the accent hit.
- **Subline** below, small, Bone at ~70% opacity: `Custom websites. Nothing off the shelf.`
- **Logo/wordmark** bottom-center or top-left: `DAMN`.
- Optional: thin violet→cyan particle/geometry motif echoing the site, low opacity, behind text.

## Type
- Match site font. Headline 120–160 pt, subline 32–40 pt.
- Left-align or center — pick one, stay consistent.

## Copy rules (hard constraint)
- No fake claims — no client counts, no invented metrics, no testimonials.
- Never "new startup". Tone confident, established.
- Qualitative only. Let work speak.

## Caption (post text)
> DAMN GOOD websites. Custom-built, no templates. Inquiries open — link in bio.

Hashtags: keep ≤5, relevant (`#webdesign #customwebsite #studio`).

## Accessibility
- Headline contrast vs black: Bone passes. Gradient text — keep on the lighter/cyan end over black for contrast.
- Don't put critical text only on the gradient's violet end (low contrast on black).

## Export
- `damn-ig-launch.png`, 1080², sRGB.
- Story variant later if needed (1080 × 1920) — same elements, vertical. Skip until asked.
